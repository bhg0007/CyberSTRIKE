Cyber Hygiene & Best Practices

A comprehensive high school-level lesson on cybersecurity awareness and safe digital practices.

Learning Objectives

  • Define cyber hygiene and explain its importance.
  • Recognize common cybersecurity threats such as phishing and malware.
  • Develop and implement strong password management strategies.
  • Describe the impact of digital footprints and safe social media practices.
  • Connect cyber hygiene to career readiness and digital citizenship.

Major Concepts

  • Cybersecurity fundamentals
  • Online safety and threat detection
  • Social media best practices
  • Ethical use of technology
  • Digital footprints and reputation

Key Terms

  • Cyber Hygiene: Safe practices to protect devices and personal data.
  • Phishing: Deceptive tactics to obtain private information.
  • Malware: Software designed to harm or exploit systems.
  • Digital Footprint: Online trail of a user’s activity.
  • CIA Triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability in cybersecurity.
  • Password Management: Techniques for creating and storing strong passwords.
  • Location Services: Device-based tracking that can expose private data.
  • Two-Factor Authentication: Dual-layer security for accounts.
  • Social Engineering: Manipulation to gain confidential data.

Detailed Lesson Outline

  1. Introduction (10-15 min): Class discussion on cybersecurity. Present key terms and real-world relevance.
  2. Instruction (20-25 min): Guided exploration of phishing, passwords, updates, and social media risks.
  3. Activity (15 min): Interactive "Cyber Threat or Not?" scenarios for student decision-making.
  4. Reflection (10 min): Students journal two specific actions they’ll take to improve cyber hygiene.
  5. Assessment: Exit ticket with three questions and participation in discussion and scenarios.
  6. Extension (optional): Research current cyberattacks or explore cybersecurity careers via UAH CCRE or RING course.
